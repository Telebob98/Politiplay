Dear Friends and Family,

OK, I've been working on something I can't stop thinking about, and now I have to share it. As people who've put up with me through the years, I'm asking you to hear me out again. I think this is a damn good idea and I hope you agree. If you don't, you're crazy — but that's another matter entirely.

It's called Politiplay. Fantasy football for global politics. You draft 11 real-world public figures onto your squad — presidents, senators, chaos agents, tech billionaires — and score points when they make news. Elections. Legislation. Scandals. Viral moments. Weekly contests called Sweeps. We treat life like a game we play anyway — Politiplay just keeps score.

"Pick the ones who matter. Win the week. Prizes, praise, and the knowledge that you know what the hell is going on."

If you follow fantasy sports, you already understand the mechanics. If you're a news junkie or politically aware, this game was made for you. If you're both — you've been waiting for this without knowing it.

And underneath the game is something more serious: a crowd-sourced political intelligence platform. Every draft pick and Captain selection is a data point. At scale, that data — who the public trusts, who they're backing, who they drop when the news turns — is worth real money to pollsters, newsrooms, and researchers. The game drives the data. The data drives the business.
