## One-Sentence Pitch

> **Draft world leaders. Score when they make news. Win your Sweep.**

---

## 1. Concept Overview

Politiplay is a fantasy politics league modeled on fantasy football — not a simulation, not a prediction game, not a civics quiz. Players draft real-world political figures onto a squad, earn points when those figures make news, and compete in weekly contests called **Sweeps**.

**The design metaphor:** FantasyCongress.com is a chatroom. Politiplay is Twitter.
Fast, visual, globally scannable, built for shareability and daily engagement.

**What Politiplay really is:** A game on the surface. A crowd-sourced political intelligence platform underneath. The game drives the data. The data drives the business.

**Primary audience:** Anyone who consumes political news — news junkies, political enthusiasts, journalists tracking trends, pollsters and statisticians, political operatives and campaign staff, academic researchers, and students of politics, international relations, and civics. Every mechanic must be explainable in 60 seconds but the platform serves professionals as deeply as casual players.

**Inspired by:** FantasyCongress.com — borrowed mechanics include weekly lineup lock, snake/auction draft, and an educator tier. Politiplay extends all of these and adds global scope, momentum mechanics, shareable moment cards, a dual Sweep structure, and a data intelligence layer that makes the platform valuable far beyond the game itself.

**Phase 2 vision:** Once the platform, player base, and data credibility are established — a cash prize competitive layer operated offshore under appropriate legal jurisdiction, modeled on DraftKings/FanDuel applied to politics.

## 2. The Core Loop — 3 Steps

1. **Draft your team** — Pick 11 world leaders and political figures
2. **They make news** — You score points based on real-world events
3. **Win the Sweep** — Most points wins the week

---

## 3. The Weekly Sweep

The Sweep is the core engagement rhythm. It runs Monday to Sunday every week.

| Day | What happens |
|---|---|
| **Monday** | Lineups lock. Sweep begins. |
| **Mon – Fri** | Events score live. Feed updates daily. |
| **Weekend** | Bonus events possible. Waiver window opens. |
| **Sunday** | Sweep winner announced and share card generated. |

Weekly scoring is **variable** — tied to real-world news. A quiet week produces a tight low-scoring Sweep. A week with a major election, coup attempt, or breaking scandal can push scores dramatically higher. This variability is intentional and authentic.

---

## 4. Your Squad — 11 Players, No Bench

Every player on your squad scores every week. There is no bench. All 11 slots matter.

### Position Slots

| Position | Slots | Points multiplier | Who qualifies |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Captain** | 1 | **2×** | Any positional player — your biggest weekly bet. Locked Monday, no swaps. |
| **Head of State** | 2 | 1× | Presidents, prime ministers, monarchs |
| **Legislator** | 2 | 1× | Senators, MPs, ministers |
| **Opposition Leader** | 2 | 1× | Challengers, rivals, critics — scores on chaos |
| **World Body Rep** | 1 | 1× + multilateral bonus | UN, EU, NATO, WTO, IMF leaders |
| **Wildcard** | 2 | **1.5×** | Chaos agents only — see Section 5 |

**Total: 11 players. No bench. Everyone contributes every Sweep.**

### Multiplier Strategy

The multiplier ladder creates three distinct playstyles:

- **Safe player** — stable Heads of State, reliable Captain, avoid Wildcards
- **Chaos player** — both Wildcard slots filled with volatile figures, high-risk Captain
- **Balanced player** — one safe Wildcard (ex-leader), one risky one, steady Captain

---

## 5. Wildcard Players — Chaos Agents

Wildcards are political kibbitzers — figures who are not currently in official positions but who muscle their way into the news cycle anyway. They score at **1.5×** — high risk, high reward.

### Who qualifies as a Wildcard?

- Former leaders / ex-heads of state still making news
- Celebrity and media figures who enter political discourse
- Activists and protest leaders
- Tech billionaires and oligarchs (Musk, Bezos, Zuckerberg, etc.)
- Fired or disgraced officials who won't stay quiet
- Rogue candidates and fringe politicians

### Wildcard pool management

The Wildcard pool is **editorially curated** — approximately 60 figures at launch, refreshed weekly based on who is actually in the news cycle. Figures are added and retired by the editorial team.

## 6. Contest Structure — Regions and Sweeps

### Pick your arena

Players choose which regional league to compete in, or enter the global league.

| Region | Depth |
|---|---|
| **USA** | Deep — see Section 8 |
| **EU + UK** | Deep — see Section 8 |
| **Asia** | Curated top leaders |
| **Latin America** | Curated top leaders |
| **Global** | Open — draft anyone from any region |

**Middle East and Russia:** Parked for future seasons. Data reliability and state propaganda concerns make fair scoring difficult at launch.

**China:** Included in Asia but scored on internationally verifiable events only (UN votes, trade deals, diplomatic meetings). No domestic approval data used.

### Roster rules by league type

**Regional league:** Captain (1) + Head of State (2) + Legislator (2) + Opposition Leader (2) + World Body Rep (1) + one additional regional starter (1) = 9 regional players, plus 2 Wildcards from any region = 11 total.

**Global league:** No restriction — draft any 11 figures from anywhere in the world.

### The Dual Sweep

Every week, players compete simultaneously in two contests:

- **Regional Sweep** — win your region (USA, EU, Asia, LatAm)
- **Global Sweep** — win the worldwide leaderboard

**Double Sweep:** Winning both in the same week is the rarest and most prestigious achievement in the game. It generates a special shareable card and is prominently celebrated.

### League types

| League | Description |
|---|---|
| **Global league** | Open entry, public leaderboard, anyone can join |
| **Friend league** | Invite-only, private, commissioner-run |
| **Class league** | Teacher sets it up, free educator tier |

## 7. Scoring System

### Base scoring by position (before multipliers)

| Event | Head of State | Legislator | Opposition | Wildcard |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Election win | +30 pts | +20 pts | +25 pts | +20 pts |
| Election loss | −15 pts | −10 pts | −5 pts | −10 pts |
| Major policy win | +20 pts | +15 pts | +10 pts | +15 pts |
| Minor bill / policy move | +8 pts | +10 pts | +5 pts | +8 pts |
| Approval rating rise (+3pts+) | +8 pts | +6 pts | +6 pts | +5 pts |
| Approval rating drop (−3pts+) | −8 pts | −6 pts | −4 pts | −5 pts |
| Treaty / alliance signed | +15 pts | +8 pts | +5 pts | +12 pts |
| Major speech / viral moment | +10 pts | +8 pts | +10 pts | +12 pts |

**Notes:**
- Opposition leaders lose less on election defeat (expected to lose) and gain more on viral moments (disruption is their job)
- Wildcards peak on treaties and viral moments — chaos agents thrive when the news cycle gets loud

### World Body Rep — special scoring

| Event | Points |
|---|---|
| Multilateral event (UN vote, G7, NATO summit) | +15 pts + 5 bonus |
| Global crisis response (WHO, IMF emergency) | +20 pts + 5 bonus |
| Failed summit / bloc breakdown / walkout | −15 pts |

### Bonus scoring situations

| Bonus | Trigger | Points |
|---|---|---|
| **Breaking news bonus** | Major unexpected event — coup, assassination attempt, snap election | +15 pts on top of base |
| **Streak bonus** | Figure scores points 3+ consecutive weeks | +5 pts per week sustained |
| **Regional dominance bonus** | Your figure is #1 story in their home region that week | +8 pts |
| **Rival penalty** | Your figure directly loses to another player's figure | −5 pts (both still score base) |

## 8. Bad News Department — Full Penalty Card

Penalties are **tiered** — a gaffe is not the same as an indictment. Penalties are **self-contained** — they do not create bonus points for opponents.

### The Three Tiers

- **Tier 1 — Gaffe:** Embarrassing but survivable
- **Tier 2 — Scandal:** Serious, damages standing
- **Tier 3 — Catastrophe:** Career-defining, possibly terminal

### Penalty table (base points before position multipliers)

| Bad news event | Tier | Base penalty | Captain / Wildcard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gaffe — public embarrassment | Tier 1 | −5 pts | −10 / −8 pts |
| Caught in a lie — public fact-check | Tier 1 | −8 pts | −16 / −12 pts |
| Approval rating crash (5pt+ drop) | Tier 1 | −8 pts | −16 / −12 pts |
| Major policy failure or reversal | Tier 2 | −12 pts | −24 / −18 pts |
| Party rebellion — own side turns | Tier 2 | −12 pts | −24 / −18 pts |
| Lost vote of no confidence | Tier 2 | −15 pts | −30 / −22 pts |
| Scandal breaks (affair, corruption, leak) | Tier 2 | −18 pts | −36 / −27 pts |
| Impeachment proceedings begin | Tier 3 | −25 pts | −50 / −38 pts |
| Criminal indictment or arrest | Tier 3 | −25 pts | −50 / −38 pts |
| Forced resignation or removal | Tier 3 | −30 pts | −60 / −45 pts |

### The Boris / Trump Rule

Volatile figures score big on good weeks and crash on bad weeks. A Captain who gets indicted loses 50 pts — potentially wiping out two good weeks. This is intentional. Drafting a chaos agent as Captain is a genuine gamble. Smart players hedge: stable Captain, volatile Wildcard — not both maxed on chaos.

### Stacking penalties

Multiple bad events in the same week stack fully (e.g. scandal + approval crash = −18 + −8 = −26 pts). However, a **weekly penalty cap of −60 pts per figure** protects players from total wipeout in a single catastrophic week.

### The Redemption Rule

If a figure scores positive points in the 2 weeks following a Tier 2 or Tier 3 penalty, they earn a **+10 pt redemption bonus**. Comeback kids are worth holding. This mirrors real politics — players are rewarded for smart roster patience.

## 9. The Player Pool — ~1,000 Figures Total

### Pool architecture — tiered activation

All figures exist in the database and score automatically via the pipeline. Not all are visible in the draft room — they are tiered by news presence score:

| Tier | Count | Draft room? | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier A** | ~300 | Always visible | High news presence, score weekly |
| **Tier B** | ~200 | Visible | Moderate presence, score regularly |
| **Tier C** | ~400 | Waiver wire only | Low presence — auto-promoted when pipeline detects news spike |
| **P+P only** | ~100 | Peace + Progress league only | NGO leaders, activists, Nobel laureates |

**The auto-promotion mechanic:** When a Tier C figure suddenly appears in 3+ data feeds simultaneously, the pipeline auto-promotes them to Tier B and surfaces them on the waiver wire. This IS the sleeper pick mechanic — the algorithm finds obscure figures before players do.

**Draft-visible at launch:** ~500 figures. Total database: ~1,000. KISS preserved — new players never see all 1,000 at once.

## 10. Automated Data Pipeline

### Layer 1 — Raw data sources (7 independent feeds)

| Feed | API endpoint | Scores |
|---|---|---|
| **GovInfo.gov** | api.govinfo.gov | US bills, votes, floor speeches |
| **Hansard API** | hansard.parliament.uk | UK votes, speeches, divisions |
| **EU Parliament open data** | data.europarl.europa.eu | EU votes, legislation, MEP activity |
| **Wikidata** | query.wikidata.org/sparql | Political figure metadata, roles, positions |
| **GDELT Project** | api.gdeltproject.org | Global news events, geopolitical incidents |
| **Reuters / AP / Politico** | newsapi.org + direct feeds | Breaking news, headlines, event confirmation |
| **Morning Consult / YouGov** | morningconsult.com/api | Weekly approval ratings, polling data |

### Layer 2 — Threshold engine (auto-classification)

No human editorial decisions for Tier 1 or Tier 2 events. Scoring triggers automatically when source thresholds are met:

| Tier | Threshold | Source requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Gaffe** | 3+ major outlets report same event | Any 3 of 7 feeds |
| **Tier 2 — Scandal** | 5+ outlets + government source confirms | 5 of 7 feeds + 1 official API |
| **Tier 3 — Catastrophe** | Auto-triggers at 5+ feeds, editorial confirms within 2hrs | 5 of 7 feeds + editorial sign-off |

### Layer 3 — Scoring engine

Once an event is classified, points are applied instantly to all affected roster spots across all leagues. A feed card is generated showing the event, point value, and all source links for public verification.

### Layer 4 — Dispute window

Every auto-scored event has a **24-hour dispute window**. If 10%+ of affected players flag the same event, it escalates to editorial review within 24 hours. All source links are permanently public. This makes scoring self-policing — the community catches edge cases.

**Note:** China scored on Western-verifiable international events only. No domestic approval data.

## 11c. Peace + Progress League

A separate league type alongside Global, Regional, Friend, and Class leagues. Same Sweep rhythm, same 11-player no-bench structure. Different scoring logic, different player pool, different position slots.

### Tagline
*Draft world leaders doing the most good. Score when they advance peace, justice, climate, and humanity.*

### Position slots

| Position | Slots | Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| Captain | 1 | 2× |
| Peacemaker | 2 | 1× |
| Climate Champion | 2 | 1× |
| Justice Advocate | 2 | 1× |
| Humanitarian | 1 | 1× |
| Changemaker Wildcard | 2 | 1.5× |

### Scoring — impact over drama

| Event | Points |
|---|---|
| Ceasefire or peace agreement signed | +35 pts |
| Major climate agreement or legislation | +30 pts |
| Humanitarian aid deployed or crisis resolved | +25 pts |
| Human rights ruling or justice reform | +25 pts |
| Anti-corruption conviction or transparency win | +20 pts |
| Education or health policy win | +15 pts |
| Poverty reduction or equity milestone | +15 pts |
| Peace talks stall or collapse | −20 pts |
| Climate commitment broken or reversed | −20 pts |
| Inaction — figure does nothing of note | −5 pts |

**The inaction penalty is the key design innovation.** In the main game, doing nothing is neutral. In Peace + Progress, doing nothing costs points. Progress is the expectation, not the exception.

### Player pool (~100 exclusive figures)

Curated subset of figures known for positive impact — NGO leaders, UN special envoys, activists, Nobel laureates, humanitarian figures. Not visible in main game draft room.

Sample figures: António Guterres (UN), Malala Yousafzai, Greta Thunberg, Jacinda Ardern, Tedros Adhanom (WHO), Pope Leo XIV.

### B2B value — unique to this league

Draft patterns reveal which leaders the public associates with positive global impact — a dataset worth real money to NGOs, development banks, ESG investors, and philanthropic foundations.

## 14. Key Design Principles

1. **KISS** — Keep It Simple Stupid. Every mechanic must be explainable in 60 seconds regardless of the player's background.
2. **Sweeps give daily gratification** — players check in every day Mon–Fri, tension builds to Sunday announcement.
3. **Variable scoring keeps it real** — quiet weeks are tight races, big news weeks are explosive. The game mirrors the world.
4. **Risk is strategy** — the Boris/Trump problem is a feature. Volatile figures are tempting and dangerous.
5. **Everyone on your squad matters** — no bench means no dead weight. Every pick counts every week.
6. **Shareability is the growth engine** — every Sweep win, Double Sweep, and dramatic scoring moment generates a card worth posting.
7. **Editorial integrity** — Middle East and Russia excluded at launch due to data reliability. China scored on verifiable international events only. Human editorial layer reviews Tier 3 events.
8. **The game drives the data** — every player action is a data point. At scale the platform becomes a crowd-sourced political intelligence engine with real B2B value.
9. **Built for everyone** — casual news junkies play for fun. Journalists, pollsters, and researchers extract signal. Political operatives track trends. The same platform serves all of them.

Speculation about future roles does not affect current slot assignment.
